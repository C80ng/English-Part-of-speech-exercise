Understanding Parts of Speech

In English, every word has a role. These roles are called Parts of Speech. Learning them helps us understand how sentences work. There are 8 common parts of speech:

1. Noun

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.
Example: Rina, Jakarta, pencil, lovecat, mountain, freedom, water, teacher, music, computer, happiness, friend, and time.

2. Pronoun

A pronoun replaces a noun to avoid repetition.
Example: I, you, they, we, she, it,

3. Verb

A verb shows an action or a state of being.
Example: walk, eat, is, sleep, run, eat, swim, think, create, discover, become, help, study, laugh.

4. Adjective

An adjective describes a noun or pronoun.
Example: happy, big, blue, interesting, loud, soft, brave, shiny, delicious, careful, beautiful, tall, red.

5. Adverb

An adverb describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb.
Example: quickly, very, well, , silently, fortunately, often, here, almost, easily, always, outside, soon, well.

6. Preposition

A preposition shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word.
Example: in, on, front, behind, under, above, beside, between, around, at.

7. Conjunction

A conjunction connects words or groups of words.
Example: for, and, nor, but, also, yet, so, because, although, while, since, unless, until, as.

8. Interjection

An interjection shows emotion or sudden feeling.
Example: Wow!, Oh!, Hey.,!Hey!, Oops!, Ah!, Hmm., Phew!, Yikes!, Hurray!, Shhh!, Bravo!.
